Biography

I will join the School of Data Science in the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen as an Associate Professor in February 2021. Previously, I was a Research Associate in the School of Computer Science and Engineering, the University of New South Wales (UNSW), working with Prof. Xuemin Lin. I received my PhD degree from Department of Computer Science in the University of Hong Kong (HKU) in 2017, advised by Prof. Reynold Cheng. My research interests mainly focus on querying, mining, and analytics of big data (particularly big graph/spatial data). Till now, I have published around 50 papers in the areas of database and data mining, including One of the Best Papers in SIGMOD 2020 (a world flagship conference in database areas), and most of them are published in top-tier conferences (e.g., PVLDB, SIGMOD, ICDE, and IJCAI) and journals (e.g., VLDBJ and TKDE). I am an editorial board member of the journal of Information & Processing Management (IPM). I have also served as program committee members for several top conferences (e.g., ICDE, AAAI, and IJCAI) and invited reviewers for top journals (e.g., TKDE, VLDBJ, and TOC) in the areas of database and data mining.

Research Interests

My general research area is querying, mining, and analytics of big data. Currently, I am working on the following research topics over the big graph data and big spatial data:

  • Graph area: cohesive subgraph search, graph embedding, graph neural network, similarity search
  • Spatial area: geo-social network queries, keyword search, trajectory computing
